Is Australia's goal of vaccinating the entire adult population by October achievable?

For the Australian government to meet its goal of more than 45m doses of Covid-19 by the end of October, it will have to average a rate higher than most countries are achieving.



Although the government’s timelines and vaccination targets are vague, a Guardian Australia analysis of the known figures suggests an average of 180,000 doses administered daily would be needed. Only a couple of countries so far have exceeded an equivalent daily vaccination rate.



But some experts think this is achievable – especially once the AstraZeneca vaccine rolls out next month and vaccinations are administered at more than a handful of hubs.
